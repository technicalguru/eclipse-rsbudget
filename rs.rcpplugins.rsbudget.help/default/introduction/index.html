Introduction

RsBudget was designed and implemented to support controlling your private financial records. Its main purpose is to let you gain and keep control over your financial health. This chapter explains basic principles and terms so you can use the software efficiently.

The General Planning

The foundation of controlling is the plan. That's why RsBudget introduces a General Plan. This plan states what recurring transactions you expect and when they will occur. This General Plan can be easily deducted from your bank's monthly account statements. So here is an example of how such a General Plan could look like:

Transaction Text Recurrence Amount
Salary monthly 1,500.00 EUR
Rent monthly -700.00 EUR
Health Insurance monthly -100.00 EUR
Fuel monthly approx. -100.00 EUR
Food monthly approx. -200.00 EUR
Car Insurance yearly
(January)		 -250.00 EUR
Period Balance monthly 400.00 EUR
Period Balance yearly 4.550.00 EUR
Average Balance monthly 379.17 EUR

The example plan above consists of six different items. The first three, "Salary" and "Rent" and "Health Insurance", are single transactions that occur each month. The last one, "Car Insurance" is a single transaction that occurs every year in January. All four's primary characteristic is that there will be only one such transaction in the given period.

"Fuel" and "Food" are different. They can occur multiple times in a given period. That's why they are called Budgets. Each individual transaction belonging to a Budget adds up to it.

Finally, the General Plan forms the foundation of a Profit/Loss planning. The General Plan tells you whether your expenses in a period are funded by respective income. RsBudget will calculate the figures "Monthly Balance, "Yearly Balance" and "Average Balance" automatically. Those figures tell you your overall financial health.

Monthly Planning

Once you have created your General Plan, it is time to track your expenses. RsBudget organizes your expenses into individual months. Each month, e.g. December 2013, will have assigned a specific plan. This Monthly Plan will be created out of the General Plan. The General Plan created earlier would result in this Monthly Plan:

Transaction Text Type Planned Amount Actual Amount
Opening Balance 0.00 EUR 0.00 EUR
Salary Transaction 1,500.00 EUR
Rent Transaction -700.00 EUR
Health Insurance Transaction -100.00 EUR
Fuel Budget -100.00 EUR
Food Budget -200.00 EUR
Closing Balance 400.00 EUR 0.00 EUR

The "Car Insurance" is not listed here as it occurs each year in January only.

Based on this plan, RsBudget now tracks your transactions and projects where your account balance will be (Column "Planned Amount"). The projection will be updated when you add actual transactions:

Transaction Text Type Planned Amount Actual Amount
Opening Balance 0.00 EUR 0.00 EUR
Salary Transaction 1,500.00 EUR
Rent Transaction -700.00 EUR -600,00 EUR
Health Insurance Transaction -100.00 EUR
Fuel Budget -100.00 EUR -50.00 EUR
Food Budget -200.00 EUR -30.00 EUR
Closing Balance 500.00 EUR -680.00 EUR

The situation above already had "Rent" and some "Fuel" and "Food" expenses happened. The actual balance of the month is -680.00 EUR because this reflects the total of this expenses.

The "Planned Closing Balance" now states 500.00 EUR. This figures calculates because the "Rent" did not occur with -700.00 EUR as planned but with -600.00 EUR. As RsBudget assumes this to be a single expense in this period, it adds the difference to the projection.

Likewise, "Food" and "Fuel" occurred with -80.00 EUR. But as they are Budgets, it is assumed that more of such transactions will occur. The difference will not be added to the projection until the month is closed.
